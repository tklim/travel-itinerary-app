BARCELONA Spain 29 May – 1 Jun
Fri 29 May ✈ Arrival + Gothic Quarter
FoodCulture
▼
08:00
Arrive El Prat Airport (BCN)
Take the Aerobus (€6.75/person) to Plaça Catalunya — fastest, no stops. Drop bags at hotel/apartment.
10:30
La Boqueria Market
Barcelona's iconic covered market on Las Ramblas. Grab fresh fruit, jamón, local olives and a quick market breakfast. Arrive early before crowds peak.
12:00
Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic) stroll
Wander the medieval labyrinth — Plaça Reial, Barcelona Cathedral, Pont del Bisbe. Free and unmissable.
14:00
Lunch at El Xampanyet
Classic cava bar in El Born. Order the house cava, patatas bravas, croquetas and pa amb tomàquet. Budget ~€20–25pp.
16:00
El Born & Picasso Museum
Explore the trendy El Born district. Visit Picasso Museum (book online in advance, ~€15pp) or browse the boutique shops nearby.
19:30
Barceloneta Beach sunset + tapas dinner
Walk to the beach for golden hour. Dinner at La Cova Fumada (locals-only spot, cash only) or Bar Electricitat for grilled seafood and vermouth.

💡 Book Picasso Museum and Sagrada Família tickets online before you travel — they sell out weeks in advance, especially in late May/June.

Sat 30 May 🏛 Gaudí Day — Sagrada Família + Park Güell
CultureOutdoor
▼
08:30
Sagrada Família (first entry)
Book the earliest slot — the morning light through the stained glass on the Nativity facade is extraordinary. Guided tour + tower access recommended (~€36pp). Allow 2 hours.
11:30
Recinte Modernista de Sant Pau
A UNESCO-listed art nouveau hospital 5 min walk from Sagrada Família — stunning architecture with far fewer crowds. ~€16pp.
13:30
Lunch in Gràcia neighbourhood
Walk or metro to Gràcia — the most authentically local barrio. Try La Pepita for creative montaditos or El Glop for hearty Catalan cuisine (~€20–30pp).
15:30
Park Güell
The ticketed Monumental Zone must be pre-booked (~€10pp). Arrive for your timed slot, enjoy the mosaic terrace and gingerbread gatehouses. Stunning city views.
18:00
Passeig de Gràcia & Casa Batlló
Walk down the famous boulevard — see Casa Batlló (exterior free, interior ~€35pp), Casa Milà/La Pedrera and the Block of Discord. World-class luxury shopping here too.
20:30
Dinner at Cervecería Catalana
Excellent tapas and pintxos on Carrer de Mallorca. Expect a queue but worth it — order the aubergine with honey and the bombas. ~€25–35pp.

💡 Barcelona's "magic hour" dinner is 9–10pm. Don't be alarmed by early empty restaurants — that's normal!

Sun 31 May ⛰ Montserrat Day Trip
OutdoorCulture
▼
08:30
Train from Plaça Espanya
Take the R5 FGC train to Monistrol de Montserrat, then the rack railway (cremallera) up the mountain. Journey ~1hr 15min. Buy the Trans Montserrat pass (~€36pp) — includes round-trip train + rack railway + funiculars.
10:00
Montserrat Monastery & Black Madonna
The 11th-century monastery perched dramatically on jagged rock spires. See the iconic La Moreneta statue. Try to catch the Escolania boys choir (Mon–Fri 1pm, Sun 12pm).
11:30
Sant Joan Funicular + hiking trail
Ride up to 1,100m and hike the ridge trail to Sant Joan hermitage (easy 30-min walk). Breathtaking panoramic views of Catalonia. Sturdy shoes essential.
13:30
Lunch on the mountain
Pick up local cheese, sausage and the famous Montserrat "monk's cheese" from the market stalls. Or dine at Restaurant de Montserrat (~€25–35pp).
15:00
Santa Cova Funicular trail
Ride down to the Santa Cova chapel trail — a dramatic cliff-side path with Gaudí-era sculptures. Only 45 mins round trip.
17:00
Return to Barcelona
Back in Barcelona by 6:30pm. Evening free — explore Montjuïc's Magic Fountain show (free, 9:30pm on weekends in summer) if energy allows.

💡 Montserrat is very popular on Sundays. Start early (8:30am train) to beat the crowds and enjoy the trails before it gets busy.

Mon 1 Jun ✈ Montjuïc Morning → Fly to Porto
OutdoorTransit
▼
09:00
Montjuïc by cable car + Castle
Take the Port Cable Car up to Montjuïc for sweeping harbour views. Visit the Castle (~€9pp) and Jardins de Laribal botanical gardens. A peaceful morning away from the crowd.
11:30
Poble Espanyol & last-minute shopping
The "Spanish Village" open-air museum houses artisan workshops — great for souvenirs. ~€14pp. Or browse the El Born boutiques for one final shop.
13:00
Lunch & head to airport
Quick lunch near hotel, collect bags, Aerobus to BCN airport. Allow 90 mins before departure.
~16:00
Fly Barcelona → Porto (OPO)
~2hr flight. From Porto airport, take the Metro Line E (purple) directly to central Porto — ~35 mins, ~€2pp. No need for a taxi.
19:00
Check in + São Bento Station visit
Drop bags (stay in Clérigos or Ribeira area). Walk 5 mins to São Bento Station — one of the world's most beautiful train stations, with 20,000 azulejo tiles. Free, open daily.
20:30
First dinner in Porto — Francesinha!
Café Santiago (Rua de Passos Manuel) is Porto's most famous Francesinha spot — the city's iconic meat-and-cheese sandwich in a spiced beer sauce. ~€15–18pp.

💡 Stay in the Clérigos or Ribeira neighbourhood in Porto — central, walkable, and you'll be close to everything.

PORTO Portugal 1–4 Jun
Tue 2 Jun 🏘 Historic Porto — Ribeira & Gaia Wine Cellars
CultureWineFood
▼
09:00
Porto Food & Wine Morning Tour
Join the "Porto Food & Vintage Port Wine Tour" from Mercado do Bolhão — 3.5 hours, 13 tastings, one of the highest-rated food tours in Europe. Book ahead (~€75pp).
12:30
Ribeira District & Porto Cathedral (Sé)
Walk the colourful riverside neighbourhood. Climb up to the 12th-century Cathedral — free entry, €3 for the azulejo cloister. Best views of the Douro River from the terrace.
14:30
Cross Dom Luís I Bridge → Vila Nova de Gaia
Walk across the iconic iron bridge (upper deck for the best views). Head into the famous Port wine cellars on the south bank — Taylor's, Graham's and Sandeman are all excellent (~€15–25pp with tasting).
17:00
Douro River Six Bridges Cruise
Board a rabelo boat for the classic "6 Bridges Cruise" — ~1 hour sailing under all the city's bridges from a water-level view. €15–18pp, boats run 11am–4pm.
18:30
Gaia Cable Car + sunset viewpoint
Ride the Teleférico de Gaia up to Miradouro da Serra do Pilar (~€6pp) for the best sunset panorama of Porto. Photographers' dream.
20:30
Dinner at Mercado Bom Sucesso
Buzzy food hall with multiple vendors — great for groups where everyone wants something different. Octopus, bacalhau, petiscos (Portuguese tapas). ~€20–30pp.

💡 Port wine is 20% alcohol — it sneaks up on you! Pace yourselves during the morning food tour and afternoon cellar visits.

Wed 3 Jun 🍇 Douro Valley Day Trip
OutdoorWineFood
▼
08:00
Depart Porto for Douro Valley
Book a guided day tour from Porto (~€70–90pp) — includes transport, wine tastings and lunch. Alternatively, take the scenic Douro train line from São Bento to Pinhão (~2.5hrs, ~€12pp each way) — one of Europe's most beautiful rail journeys.
10:30
Quinta do Crasto or Quinta do Vallado
Visit one of the region's top organic quintas (wine estates). Tour the terraced vineyards, learn about the harvest and taste Douro whites, reds and vintage Port. The scenery — UNESCO-listed terraced hillsides — is jaw-dropping.
13:00
Lunch with Douro River views
Traditional Portuguese lunch at a quinta restaurant — expect bacalhau à brás, roasted lamb and local wines. Usually included in guided tours or ~€25–35pp independently.
15:00
Village of Pinhão & river cruise
Explore the charming Pinhão village (famous for its azulejo-decorated train station). Optional short boat ride on the Douro through the valley.
17:30
Return to Porto
Back in Porto by 7–7:30pm. A more relaxed evening — perfect for a quiet dinner.
20:00
Fado show + dinner
End your Porto stay with a Fado performance — Casa da Mariquinhas or Ideal Clube de Fado are authentic venues. Deeply moving Portuguese soulful music. ~€15–20pp entry.

💡 Book the Douro Valley tour at least 2 weeks ahead — the best quintas and guided tours sell out fast in June.

Thu 4 Jun 📚 Porto Morning → Back to Barcelona
CultureShoppingTransit
▼
09:00
Livraria Lello — the famous bookshop
One of the world's most beautiful bookshops — a neo-Gothic 1906 masterpiece. Queue early to get good photos. Entry €5pp (redeemable against purchase).
10:30
Clérigos Tower + Palácio da Bolsa
Climb the 225 steps of the Baroque Clérigos Tower for 360° city views (~€5pp). Then visit the stunning Arab Room inside Palácio da Bolsa (~€5pp).
12:00
Rua de Santa Catarina shopping
Porto's main pedestrian shopping street — mix of local boutiques, Portuguese ceramics, cork products and international brands. Great for last-minute souvenirs.
13:00
Farewell lunch — Bacalhau à Brás
Try the classic salted cod dish at Cantinho do Avillez or Brasão Aliados. ~€20–25pp. Then metro to airport (Line E, 35 mins).
~18:00
Fly Porto → Barcelona
~2hr flight back. Check in to your Barcelona hotel/apartment for the final two nights.
21:00
Final Barcelona night out
Head to El Born or Poble Sec for tapas and cocktails. Bodega Sepúlveda, Bar Calders or a classic vermouth bar in the Raval neighbourhood.
Fri 5 Jun 🌅 Final Morning — Departure by 11am
FoodTransit
▼
07:30
Final breakfast in Barcelona
Café croissant and cortado at a local granjería. If staying near Eixample, try Federal Café or Alsur Café for a proper last morning.
09:00
Collect bags & Aerobus to BCN airport
Aerobus departs from Plaça Catalunya every 5–10 minutes. Journey ~35 mins. Arrive airport with 90 minutes to spare before any departure.
